# Gemini

Here is the complete framework broken down into a structured list format. This avoids the grid formatting of a table so it is much easier to copy and paste into your notes, a document, or an email.
## 1. System Visibility (Seeing the System)

### Value Stream Mapping (VSM)
   * Its True Purpose: To map the sequence of steps from "concept to cash" and see where work actually waits.
   * What Happens If It's Missing: You are blind to the overall system and will optimize individual tasks instead of the whole chain.
### Gemba (The Actual Place)
   * Its True Purpose: To observe how work actually happens on the ground, rather than how a manager thinks it happens.
   * What Happens If It's Missing: You design beautiful, logical processes on paper that fail immediately because they don't match human reality.
## 2. Throughput Control (Managing the Speed)
### Theory of Constraints (TOC)
   * Its True Purpose: To identify the single weakest link (the bottleneck) that dictates the speed of the entire system.
   * What Happens If It's Missing: You waste time speeding up non-bottlenecks, which just piles up more unfinished work in front of the actual bottleneck.
### Work in Progress (WIP) Limits
   * Its True Purpose: To stop multitasking and force the team to finish old work before starting new work.
   * What Happens If It's Missing: People feel insanely busy, but nothing ever actually gets delivered. Cycle times skyrocket.
### Upstream Triage / Queue Management
   * Its True Purpose: To aggressively filter and prioritize ideas before they are allowed to enter the delivery system.
   * What Happens If It's Missing: The team gets buried under a mountain of chaotic, half-baked feature requests and demands.
### 3. Quality & Alignment (Steering the Ship)
### Definition of Value (Feedback Loops)
   * Its True Purpose: To ensure that the fast-moving items coming out of your system actually solve a real problem for a customer.
   * What Happens If It's Missing: You become incredibly efficient at building the wrong things faster than ever before.
### Explicit Policies (DoD / DoR)
* Its True Purpose: To create clear, shared agreements on what "Ready" (DoR) and "Done" (DoD) actually mean for a task.
* What Happens If It's Missing: People waste time arguing over quality standards, or tasks get pushed back because they were incomplete.
## 4. The Human Culture (Powering the Machine)
### Psychological Safety
   * Its True Purpose: To create an environment where team members can point out bottlenecks, admit mistakes, and halt bad work without fear.
   * What Happens If It's Missing: People hide delays, cover up defects, and fake their metrics to look good, quietly poisoning the system.
### Exploiting Slack
   * Its True Purpose: To deliberately leave breathing room in the system so workers aren't at 100% capacity.
   * What Happens If It's Missing: The system becomes incredibly brittle. The moment an emergency happens, the entire flow grinds to a halt.
### Continuous Improvement (Kaizen)
   * Its True Purpose: To use data from retrospectives and flow metrics to constantly tweak and evolve the system.
   * What Happens If It's Missing: Your workflow becomes a rigid bureaucracy. A system that doesn't evolve eventually decays.
